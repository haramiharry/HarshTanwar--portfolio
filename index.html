The Sustainability & Waste Optimizer
ESG · Supply Chain · Fashion Overproduction
 ESG / Supply Chain
Fashion overproduction costs the industry $500B/year. Demand-sensing models cut excess inventory by 20–35%, markdown rates by 18%, and Scope 3 emissions by 12%.
Expand ↓
The Problem
Fashion overproduction is one of the industry's most expensive and environmentally damaging failures. The numbers are damning: the global fashion industry produces 100 billion garments per year — approximately 30–40% go unsold. The Ellen MacArthur Foundation estimates overproduction costs brands $500B annually in lost revenue from markdowns and deadstock. McKinsey's 2023 State of Fashion report identified "inventory management" as the #1 operational risk cited by fashion executives.

The root cause is structural: buying decisions are made 9–12 months in advance using historical sell-through data and intuition-based trend forecasting. By the time collections hit the floor, the market has moved.

The Optimizer Framework
A four-layer demand-sensing and ESG alignment system aligned with modern supply chain operations:

Layer 1 — Real-Time Demand Signals: Replace historical buy sheets with live sell-through data feeds, social trend velocity scores (Heuritech, Trendalytics), and regional weather/event triggers integrated into the planning tool.

Layer 2 — Dynamic MOQ Negotiation: Implement rolling 6-week order windows with factory partners instead of season-locked minimums. Requires pre-negotiated flexibility clauses — a change management exercise as much as a commercial one.

Layer 3 — ESG KPI Dashboard: Track Scope 3 emissions per SKU, markdown rate by category, and deadstock-to-donation ratio — reported at buyer level, not just sustainability team level.

Layer 4 — End-of-Life Protocol: Pre-defined decision tree for slow-movers: reprice → partner channel → charity donation → fibre recycling. Eliminates landfill as a default.

AI Trend Forecasting — The Heuritech Model
Image Recognition · Fashion Intelligence · Runway Prediction
 AI / Trend Tech
Heuritech forecasted "dotted prints" and "butter yellow" 12+ months before runway saturation. Brands using the model reduced trend-miss markdowns by up to 30%.
Expand ↓
How Heuritech Works
Heuritech, founded in Paris, built a proprietary image recognition engine that analyses over 3 million social media images per day — Instagram, Pinterest, TikTok, and street-style databases — using computer vision to detect micro-trends at the garment attribute level: silhouette, colour family, print motif, fabric texture, and styling cues.

Unlike traditional trend agencies (WGSN, Peclers) that rely on human curators attending trade shows, Heuritech's algorithm detects statistical acceleration — the rate at which a visual attribute is appearing more frequently in the wild — before it is consciously noticed by the market. This gives brands a 6–18 month early signal window.

Commercial Application
Heuritech's clients include LVMH, Adidas, Lacoste, and Carrefour's apparel divisions. The platform delivers three outputs brands use operationally:

Trend Probability Scores: Each detected attribute receives a 12-month adoption forecast (niche → emerging → mainstream → declining) allowing buyers to stage order quantities by risk tier.

Regional Velocity Maps: The same trend travels at different speeds in Paris vs. Seoul vs. New York. Regional velocity data allows localised assortment decisions rather than global buys.

Markdown Risk Alerts: When a trend's adoption curve plateaus earlier than forecast, buyers receive an alert — enabling early price action before margin erosion.

The strategic implication for trade marketing: trend intelligence is now a pre-season commercial tool, not a post-season post-mortem.

NoCapp — Subscription Luxury Wig Brand
Blue Ocean Strategy · Brand Design · Go-To-Market · Paris School of Business · 2025
 Brand Strategy
Full brand and go-to-market concept for a subscription-based luxury wig company in France. Blue Ocean positioning across a €300M market with 10M+ potential customers. Three subscription tiers, three-channel distribution, full competitor and customer journey analysis.
Expand ↓
The Strategic Brief
NoCapp is a premium subscription-based wig company offering 100% ethically sourced human hair wigs for women and men aged 16–50 in France. The brief was to build a full brand strategy and go-to-market plan using Blue Ocean Strategy — creating a new market space rather than competing in the overcrowded synthetic wig segment.

Market Context: France's wig and hair extension market generates approximately €300M annually with 10M+ potential customers. Existing options are either synthetic and low quality, or medical-grade and sterile. No brand occupies the intersection of luxury, personalization, gender-inclusivity, and subscription convenience.

Blue Ocean Logic: Instead of competing on price or product range, NoCapp eliminates the stigma of wig-wearing by repositioning the category from "hair loss solution" to "premium lifestyle accessory" — targeting fashion consumers, medical patients, transgender and nonbinary clients, and professionals simultaneously.
Competitive Analysis & Positioning
UniWigs (International online): Wide variety, affordable, strong e-commerce. Weaknesses: inconsistent quality, no personalization, no local fitting.

Any d'Avray (French luxury, physical): High-end reputation, medical certification, physical presence. Weaknesses: no subscription, outdated digital experience, no gender inclusivity, no youth appeal.

NoCapp's gap: The only brand that combines luxury quality + subscription convenience + personalization technology + gender inclusivity + ethical sourcing — simultaneously. Neither competitor does more than two of these well.

Three subscription tiers designed: Signature (€179/mo), Haute Couture (€249/mo), Privée (€329/mo) — each tier laddering up on personalization, quality (up to Remy human hair), and service (concierge, salon fittings, dedicated stylist).
Customer Journey & Go-To-Market
Three-channel distribution: E-commerce (AI consultation quiz, virtual stylist, subscription box delivery) · Flagship stores in Paris, Lyon, Marseille (appointment or walk-in, live styling) · Sephora retail partnership (AR try-on in-store, gateway to subscription).

Customer journey mapped across 6 stages: Discovery → Evaluation → Purchase → Onboarding → Usage & Maintenance → Emotional Outcome. Pain points and aspirations identified at each stage — for example, at the emotional outcome stage, the pain is embarrassment and disconnect from personal image; the aspiration is elevated confidence and lifestyle integration.

Supply chain: Ethical hair sourcing from India, Vietnam, and Brazil · EU-based handcrafting for quality control · Colissimo/DHL fulfillment · Shopify + CRM + AR tech stack.
